(1) Any of the following is presumptive evidence that communication under this subsection was sent by either party:

a. Certificate of mailing.

b. Registered or certified mailing showing proof of delivery.

c. Delivery in person.

(2) If the parties regularly communicated via email, text, or other written electronic means, notice may be given through the same communication method that was regularly used if the party offering evidence of the communication shows that the communication was received. Evidence of receipt may include a response from the other party, a read receipt, or some other indication of confirmation of receipt.

SYNOPSIS

This Act identifies evidence that communications were sent relating to a security deposit. It also allows for the required communications to be sent electronically if it can be shown by the party utilizing electronic communications that the parties regularly communicated by a particular method and that the electronic communication was received by the other party.

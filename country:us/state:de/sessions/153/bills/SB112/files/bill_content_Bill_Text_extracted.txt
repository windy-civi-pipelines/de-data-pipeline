WHEREAS, Colonel James L. Ford, Jr. passed away on March 1, 2025, at the age of 93; and WHEREAS, he dedicated 51 years of his life to public service in the State and Delaware and his country; and WHEREAS, his career began with service in the U.S. Navy aboard an ammunitions ship during the Korean War, followed by his return to Delaware, where he joined the Newark Police Department from 1953 to 1956; and WHEREAS, Colonel Ford led a distinguished career with the Delaware State Police, holding various positions including K-9 officer, Director of Training, and ultimately Superintendent before retiring in 1976; and WHEREAS, after retiring from the State Police, he continued his dedication to law enforcement and education as an instructor and Department Chair of Criminal Justice at Delaware Technical and Community Collegeâs Terry Campus, later serving as Assistant to the President for College Relations; and WHEREAS, in 2001, Governor Ruth Ann Minner appointed Colonel Ford as Cabinet Secretary of Public Safety and Homeland Security, a role in which he served until 2004; and WHEREAS, as a member of the Association of Delaware State Police, he chaired the Council on Police Training for the State of Delaware for 20 years, helping to shape law enforcement standards and training; and WHEREAS, his leadership extended beyond law enforcement, as he played an active role in numerous criminal justice organizations, including the Delaware State Police Museum, Delaware Chief Police Foundation, Delaware Safety Council, and Delaware Sentencing Accountability Commission, while also serving two terms as Honorary Commander of Dover Air Force Base as part of their community outreach program; and WHEREAS, his academic pursuits reflected a commitment to continued learning, having graduated from the Southern Police Institute at the University of Louisville, participated in a Winterim Program in London to study the British criminal justice system, and completed a national security program with the United States Air War College at Maxwell Air Force Base; Ford, Jr. State Police Training Academy" in honor of Colonel Fordâs lifelong dedication to public service.

Â§ 1105A. Endangering the welfare of a vulnerable adult; class A misdemeanor.

(a) A person is guilty of endangering the welfare of a vulnerable adult when the person being a guardian or any other person who has assumed responsibility for the care or supervision of the vulnerable adult the person:

(1) Intentionally, knowingly, or recklessly acts in a manner likely to be injurious to the physical, mental, or moral welfare of the vulnerable adult; or

(2) Intentionally, knowingly, or recklessly does or fails to do any act, including failing to report a missing vulnerable adult, with the result that the vulnerable adult becomes a neglected or abused adult.

(c) Endangering the welfare of a vulnerable adult is a class A misdemeanor.

SYNOPSIS

This Act creates the criminal offense of endangering the welfare of a vulnerable adult. A person found guilty of endangering the welfare of a vulnerable adult is guilty of a class A misdemeanor.
